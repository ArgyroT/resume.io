My favorite book: Murder on the Orient Express

"Murder on the Orient Express" is a gripping detective novel written by Agatha Christie. Set aboard the luxurious Orient Express train, the story follows renowned detective Hercule Poirot as he investigates a perplexing murder that occurs during the train's journey. The victim, an unsavory character named Ratchett, is found dead in his compartment, stabbed multiple times. With the train stranded due to heavy snowfall, Poirot must rely on his exceptional deductive skills to unravel the complex web of clues and identify the culprit among the passengers, each with their own secrets and motives. As the investigation unfolds, Poirot encounters a diverse cast of characters, each with their own possible connection to the crime. The novel skillfully combines suspense, mystery, and psychological depth, keeping readers guessing until the surprising and satisfying conclusion. "Murder on the Orient Express" is a classic whodunit that showcases Agatha Christie's mastery of plotting, character development, and intricate storytelling, making it a timeless and beloved mystery novel.
